Radio Receiver

A simplified AM radio receiver consists of
an RLC tuned circuit containing a resistor,
capacitor, and an inductor connected in
series, as shown in the picture.

The resonant frequency of the circuit is given by the equation :

$$ f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} $$

where L is inductance in henrys (H) and C is capacitance in farads (F).

The average (rms) voltage across the resistive load varies as a function of the frequency according to the eqaution

$$ V_R ={ \frac{R}{\sqrt{R^2+(\omega L - \frac{1}{\omega C})^2}}}V_0 $$

where \( \omega \) is the result of \( \omega=2\pi f \)

For this web page, it is needed to introduce the values of the variables inthe following units:

\( V_0 \) in volts (V)

f in kilohertz (kHz)

L in milihenry (mH)

R in ohm (\( \Omega \))

C in nanofarad (nF)

L: mH

C: nF

f: kHz

R: \( \Omega \)

\( V_0 \): V

\( f_0 \): kHz

\( V_R \): V
